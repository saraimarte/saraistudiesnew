# Find an equation of the curve parametrized by a given parametrization

# Find a parametrization of a line passing through a given point and slope

<RuleBox>
<Latex formula="\left\{\begin{aligned} 
x &= x_0 + ut \\
y &= y_0 + st
\end{aligned}\right., \quad -\infty < t < \infty" />
is defined as a parametrization of the **line** passing through (x<sub>0</sub>, y<sub>o</sub>) with slope m = s/u.

Note: There can be many parametrizations of a curve because the slope can be written differently. See the example video below.  
</RuleBox>

# Find a parametrization of a circle passing through a given center and radius

<RuleBox>
<Latex formula="\left\{\begin{aligned} 
x &= x_0 + r \cdot \cos(t) \\
y &= y_0 + r \cdot \sin(t)
\end{aligned}\right., \quad 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi" />

is a parametrization of a circle with center (h,k) and radius r. 

</RuleBox>

# Find a parametrization of an ellipse passing through a given center and radius

These are really just the basic trig pythagorean identity and the first thing is equal to cos(theta) and second thing sin(theta)

<RuleBox>
<Latex formula="\left\{\begin{aligned} 
x &= x_0 + a \cdot \cos(t) \\
y &= y_0 + b \cdot \sin(t)
\end{aligned}\right., \quad 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi" />

is a parametrization of an ellipse with center (h,k), semi-major axis a, and semi-minor axis b.  

</RuleBox>
