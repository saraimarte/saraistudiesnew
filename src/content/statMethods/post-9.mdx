# F-Test

Before, we tested population means and slopes with either the z or t test.

Testing if the mean is what is is:

H<sub>o</sub>: u = 365  
H<sub>a</sub>: u ≠ 365

Testing if slope exists:

H<sub>o</sub>: B<sub>1</sub> = 0  
H<sub>a</sub>: B<sub>1</sub>≠ 0

Now, we test population variances (σ²) with the f-test.

## Two Tail f-test (Variances are different)

The following is the summary statistics that represent the starting annual salary of math and english teachers in thousands of dollars.

Sample I (math teachers): x̄<sub>1</sub> = 40.23, S<sup>2</sup><sub>1</sub> = 12.9625, n<sub>1</sub> = 14
Sample I (english teachers): x̄<sub>2</sub> = 39.20 , S<sup>2</sup><sub>2</sub> = 8.5504, n<sub>2</sub> = 16

Test whether the two population **variances** **are different**.

**A.** What are the hypothesis?  
**B.** What is the value of the test statistic? (Round 4 decimal places)  
**C.** What is the critical value for testing whether there is evidence of a difference in the variances? (Round 4 decimal places)  
**D.** There is enough evidence showing that two population variances are different. True or False?

<Solution>

**A.**

H<sub>o</sub>: σ²<sub>1</sub> = σ²<sub>2</sub>  
H<sub>a</sub>: σ²<sub>1</sub> ≠ σ²<sub>2</sub>

Key Word: "different." Different always means two tail test. Becaues it's either this or that.

**B. 1.5160**

**C. 2.9249**

**D. False** Since we fail to reject the H<sub>o</sub>, we don't have enough evidence for the H<sub>a</sub> that is H<sub>a</sub>: σ²<sub>1</sub> ≠ σ²<sub>2</sub> (that the variances _are_ different).

</Solution>

Given that σ²<sub>1</sub> is the variance of the amount of time required to reach a police officer in town B and σ²<sub>2</sub> is the variance of the amount of time needed to reach a police officer in Town A- answer the following questions.

**A.** What are the hypothesis?  
**B.** What is the value of the test statistic? (Round 4 decimal places)  
**C.** What is the critical value at the 5% level of significance? (Round 4 decimal places)  
**D.** Given α = 0.05. There is evidence showing that the variance of the amount of time required to reach a police officer in town B is greater than that in town A. True or False?

<Solution>

**A.**

**H<sub>o</sub>: σ²<sub>2</sub> ≤ σ²<sub>1</sub>**  
**H<sub>a</sub>: σ²<sub>2</sub> > σ²<sub>1</sub>**

Key word: "greater than." That means it's not either this or that so it's not a two tail test it is a one tail test.

**B. 2.0165** When they say test-statistic, and we are testing variances then they are referring to the fStat.

**C. 2.3143** Recall: Critical value for one tail test NOT two tail test.

**D. False.** Since we fail to reject the H<sub>o</sub>, we don't have enough evidence for the H<sub>a</sub> that is **H<sub>a</sub>: σ²<sub>2</sub> > σ²<sub>1</sub>.**

</Solution>

At the 5% level of signifiance, state the decision rule for testing the null hypothesis that **all groups have equal population means**. Answer the following questions.

**A.** The null hypothesis should be μ<sub>1</sub> = μ<sub>2</sub> = ... =μ<sub>5</sub>, where μ<sub>i</sub> is the population mean in group i. True or False?  
**B.** What is the test statistic? (Round 2 decimal places)  
**C.** What is the critical value? (Round 2 decimal places)  
**D.** There is sufficient evidence to conclude that there is a difference in the population means of the groups. True or false?

<Solution>

**A. True**

That is what ANOVA test is- it's simply checking if all the population means are equal to eachother. The alternative hypothesis would be H<sub>a</sub>: Not all means are equal OR At least 1 mean is different from the others.

We also know that c is the number of groups there are and for each group there will be a mean so we will compare up until u<sub>c</sub> and since the number of groups is 5 then we will compare up until the u<sub>5</sub> mean.

**B. 2.93**

**C. 2.78** Recall: Critical value for ANOVA is easy because there is only 1 since ANOVA only does two tail tests.

**D. False.** Since we fail to reject the H<sub>o</sub> which is that all means are equal, we don't have enough evidence for the H<sub>a</sub> that is **H<sub>a</sub>: Not all means are equal.**

</Solution>

# Tukey-Kramer Procedure

If you perform an ANOVA test and it tells you that there **is** a mean that is different then you can use the Tuckey-Kramer Procedure to find **which** mean is different.

# Levenue Test

The f-test is used to test if 2 population means are different from eachother.

The levenue test is used to test if _more_ than 2 population means are different.

Because ANOVA tables usually contain more than two groups, we use the Levenue test to test if the at least one population mean is different.
