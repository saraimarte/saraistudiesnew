As always, in a stats course we will work with _statistics_ (dealing with sample data) not parameters (population data).

## Measuring Central Tendency

Central Tendency tells you where the data tends to center around.

There are three ways to measure central tendency, (1) mean (2) median and (3) mode.

In this course we only cover mean and median.

Unlike Intro to Stats, here we do everything in excel so it's faster.

In the following video, i'll show you how to find the mean and the median of a dataset and why median is a better measure of central tendency.

Central Tendency is not enough to describe a dataset. There is also range, variance, standard deviation, shape and skewness.
