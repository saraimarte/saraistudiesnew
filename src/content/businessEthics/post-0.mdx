Business’ only legal and ethical responsibility is to provide a good or service at an agreed-upon price.
Places ethical constraints on the seller not to coerce, defraud, or deceive buyers.
and Implied Warranty
In selling a product, a business implicitly assures it is suitable for its purpose.
This standard shifts the burden of proof from the consumer to the producer.
Many businesses will issue a disclaimer of liability or offer an expressed and limited warranty

Caveat Emptor – Let the buyer beware, but sellers are NOT allowed to coerce, defraud or deceive.
Implied Warranty = Merchantability – Courts have moved away from Caveat Emptor to Implied Warranty.
